## FIA Framework — How to Handle Competitor Mentions

**Never attack a competitor. Reposition instead.**

| Step | What you do | What you never do |
|------|-------------|------------------|
| **F — Fact** | Acknowledge the competitor exists | Deny they're a real option |
| **I — Impact** | Ask about their specific use case with that competitor | Trash-talk features |
| **A — Act** | Reframe around your differentiated strength | Make unsupported claims |

**FIA in practice:**
> "[Competitor] is a solid choice for [what they're actually good at]. The question is whether [their specific requirement] is on your list — because that's where we're different. Can I ask what made [Competitor] come up?"

**Why this works:** You're not arguing. You're uncovering. The question shifts the conversation to their criteria, which you can then map your strengths to.

## Winning / Battling / Losing Zones

Use this structure in `knowledge/competitive/{competitor}.md` for each competitor.

```
## [Competitor Name] — Positioning Map

### Winning Zone (where we beat them)
- [Capability / use case where we're clearly stronger]
- [Customer proof point demonstrating the win]

### Battling Zone (where it's competitive — win on process)
- [Capability / use case that's close — win on POC, champion, process]
- [What to emphasize when here]

### Losing Zone (where they beat us — don't fight)
- [Capability / use case where they're genuinely stronger]
- [How to redirect the conversation away from this zone]
```

**Rule:** Never fight in the Losing Zone. Redirect to your Winning Zone with a pivot question.

## Landmine Question Anatomy

**Definition:** A discovery question that naturally surfaces your competitor's weakness without you naming them.

**Structure:**
> "How important is [capability your competitor lacks] to your evaluation?"

**Why it works:** If the buyer says "very important," they've eliminated your competitor without you saying a word. If they say "not important," you've learned they won't buy on that criterion.

**4 Generic Landmine Examples (customize per competitor):**

1. "How important is it that [capability A] works natively with [their existing stack]?"
2. "Are you planning to [scale/expand use case] beyond the initial deployment — and if so, how does [capability B] factor in?"
3. "What's your tolerance for [implementation complexity / migration time / ongoing ops overhead]?"
4. "When you evaluate options, how much weight do you put on [long-term vendor stability / enterprise support / compliance certifications]?"

**Usage rule:** Plant 1-2 landmine questions early in discovery. Don't overuse — 2 max per call or it feels scripted.

## Repositioning Language Table

When you're in the Battling Zone, use language that shifts the frame.

| Instead of saying... | Say this... |
|---------------------|-------------|
| "[Competitor] can't do X" | "The question is how important X is to your workflow — because that's a key differentiator for us." |
| "We're better than [Competitor]" | "Different teams use each of us for different things — can I ask what's driving the evaluation?" |
| "Why are you even looking at them?" | "That makes sense to have in the mix. What would make [Competitor] the obvious choice for you?" (uncover criteria) |
| "They have a lot of problems" | "We've talked to teams who moved from [Competitor]. The pattern we see is [specific limitation]. Is that a concern for you?" |
| Staying silent when they mention competitor | Always acknowledge + ask a question. Silence reads as insecurity. |

## Quick Reference: Competitive Prep Checklist

Before any meeting where a competitor is in play:

- [ ] Which competitor(s) are in the deal? (from account-brief.md Competition section)
- [ ] What zone are we in? (Winning / Battling / Losing)
- [ ] Which 1-2 landmine questions apply to this specific deal?
- [ ] What's our FIA response if they bring up [competitor] unprompted?
- [ ] Do we have a proof point from a customer who chose us over [competitor]?

If per-competitor battlecard exists: read `knowledge/competitive/{competitor}.md` before the call.
